ABOUT THE I4 CHALLENGE

Industry 4.0 is a high school level data-driven competition run by students at the University of Waterloo. The name comes from what is known as the fourth industrial revolution, involving the computerization of manufacturing and increase in big data and analytics. The idea for this competition first came in 2017, and the first competition will be run in the winter of 2019.

Interested in Sponsoring?

Our Sponsors

Oculys Health Informatics Inc. is pleased to be the inaugural sponsor of the Industry 4.0 student challenge and provide awards to the top three teams. The competition is an excellent learning vehicle for understanding the importance of acquiring raw data, turning it into information and then using the information to inform decision making about flows and processes. The Industry 4.0 concepts are applicable across many business sectors, including healthcare. Oculys is a leading innovator in healthcare informatics and predictive analytics, and has many ties with the University of Waterloo, the Department of Management Sciences specifically, and its undergraduates, graduates, and faculty.

QUESTIONS?

FAQ

I’m a high school student, how do I sign up?

If you’re interested in signing up for the competition, first get in touch with a teacher who would be willing to act as your advisor and then sign up here.

I’m a teacher, how do I sign up a team of students?

Please go to the sign up page and follow the instructions there.

My company is interested in sponsoring this competition, how do we go about that?

Please go to the Get Involved page (link) and follow the information there. We are currently looking for sponsors in the form of money, and advisors.

When is the competition?

The inaugural I4 competition will be held in Winter 2019, the exact date is still to be determined. Current plan is set course for March 2019.

Is there a prize?

Currently we are aiming for 3 prizes for the top 3 teams. The prize will consist of scholarships to The University of Waterloo for all team members, money for a STEM based team from your high school, and money for your high school in general.

What skills will I gain from this competition?

This competition focuses on building your problem solving, analytical, excel, and data analysis skills. The competition focuses on process improvement through data analytics which will give you many skills that are applicable in your future - especially if you want to do Engineering.

Do I need to be in a certain grade to sign up?

The competition is currently geared towards students in grade 11, however we are open to having students in any grade sign up.

What is management engineering?

Management Engineering program is the study, analysis, and improvement of complex systems – improving both effectiveness and efficiency.

About Us

In the Winter 2017 term, the 2021 Class of Management Engineering students started the formation of the Industry 4.0 Team. The team is advised by Professor Kenneth N. McKay, Department of Management Sciences, Faculty of Engineering, University of Waterloo.
